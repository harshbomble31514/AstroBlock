What Problem Do We Solve?

Verifiable Authenticity

We provide undeniable proof that a specific reading was generated at a specific time, linked to a specific user session.

Shareability Without Oversharing

Users can share a public link to prove a reading's existence and authenticity without exposing any private details.

Portable, User-Owned Records

The Report NFT is a durable asset in the user's wallet, independent of our app. It's a record they own forever.

How We Solve It: A Simple Split

On-Chain Proof

The Report NFT's properties store hashes, a timestamp, and a URI. This is the immutable, public proof.

Off-Chain Privacy

The full report is encrypted with AES-GCM and stored off-chain. It's completely user-controlled.

Hybrid Verification

Anyone can verify the on-chain proof. Only the owner can connect their wallet to decrypt and deep-verify the full report.

Why Blockchain At All?

Q: “Why not a normal database?”

A: A database proves ownership only if you trust us. Blockchain proves it to anyone, forever.

Q: “What does blockchain add here?”

A: Trust, timestamp, and ownership without exposing any personally identifiable information (PII).

Q: “Isn’t it overkill?”

A: We anchor only tiny hashes and URIs. The cost is cents, but the benefit is permanent, undeniable proof.

What If We Don't Use Blockchain?

No Public Verifiability

Without a public ledger, proof reverts to easily-faked screenshots. Trust remains centralized.

Users Lose Portability

Records are trapped in our database. If our server dies, user history and trust collapse.

No Neutral Timestamp

Disputes over when a reading was created become a matter of opinion versus opinion, with no objective truth.

Privacy & Security by Design

Client-Side Encryption

Encryption happens on the user's device. Keys never leave the user, and we can never decrypt their private data.

Tamper-Proof Hashing

Public on-chain hashes ensure the integrity of the off-chain data. Any modification is immediately detectable.

FAQ: Simple Answers to Complex Questions

Q: What about gas spikes?
A: Minimal on-chain data makes us resilient. We can add retries & alerts.

Q: Couldn’t we fake it off-chain?
A: No. The on-chain hash + timestamp would make any faking instantly detectable.

Q: Why does Move matter?
A: Resource safety reduces whole classes of contract bugs, making our proofs more reliable.

Q: What if a user loses their key?
A: We cannot decrypt their data. This is by design: privacy > convenience.

Q: Why not store everything on-chain?
A: For privacy and cost. We only store the absolute minimum required for public verification.
